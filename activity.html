paragraph

A paragraph is a section of writing that markis a group of sentences related to a single topic. It provides structure to a text, making it easier to read by organizing ideas into logical, coherent units. Paragraphs are used in all types of writing, from academic essays to creative stories, and are visually marked by an indent or a line break. Structure: A paragraph typically contains a topic sentence that introduces the main idea, followed by supporting sentences that provide details, examples, or explanations. It might also conclude by restating the topic sentence in different words. Purpose: The main purpose of a paragraph is to organize writing and make it more readable and understandable for the reader. It signals a new idea or thought, which helps the reader follow the author's train of thought. Unity and Coherence: All sentences within a paragraph >should support the main idea expressed in the topic sentence to maintain unity. Using connective words can link sentences and improve the flow and coherence of the paragraph. Visual cue: A paragraph is visually set apart from other text through an indent at the beginning of the first line or a blank line between paragraphs, but typically not both.
